Finding Joy during the Winter

by Patricia Araujo

As winter arrives, some other problems may come along such as the seasonal affective disorder. This disorder is still a mystery for scientists but we gather some tips that might help you overcome those feelings:

1 - Getting some morning light

Getting a morning light will help your biological clock understand that it is morning and therefore you will have more energy. Some scientists believe that the winter depression may happen due to the excess of melatonin in your body, which is the hormone that helps you fall asleep at night, working in opposite with the cortisol, which is the hormone that gives you the energy to get through the day. Getting a morning light will help your body produce the correct amount of melatonin.

Some people have shared that as they started practicing winter sports, they started to feel happier and more accomplished during the season. So, go find yourself a sport that you like to practice during that time.

3- Taking vitamin D

We have interviewed a group of people who said that when they started to drink vitamin D during the winter, they started feeling happier and having more energy. Taking vitamin D is also good for your immune system and it is actually prescript by some doctors for patients with depression.
